# Handyman — Commercial Roadmap (Lagos) (Draft)

**Prepared:** 2026-03-19  
**Launch:** April 2026  
**Launch city:** Lagos, Nigeria  
**Monthly growth budget:** ₦800,000  
**Priority categories:** Plumbing, Electrician, Hair Stylist, AC Tech, Nail Tech, General Errands, Cleaning  
**B2B:** Included from Phase 1

## 1) Commercial strategy (what we’re selling)
Handyman is a two-sided marketplace for on-demand services with trust controls (verification codes, escrow, job photos, ratings) designed to reduce disputes and improve reliability.

**Revenue model:** 10% take rate on service fee for completed jobs (5% paid by customer + 5% deducted from handyman payout).  
**North Star Metric:** Completed orders/week in Lagos with strong completion rate and repeat usage.

## 2) KPI framework (weekly tracking)
### Supply (Handymen)
- New handymen onboarded/week
- % verified (KYC-lite completed)
- % active handymen (≥1 accepted job/week)
- Acceptance rate
- Decline rate (pre-start declines)
- No-show rate (accepted/arrived but not started)

### Demand (Customers)
- New customers/week
- Orders created/week
- Completed orders/week
- 30-day repeat rate
- Satisfaction proxy (ratings / NPS-like survey)

### Marketplace health
- Time to first accept (TTFA)
- Time from accept → arrived
- Completion rate (created → completed)
- Dispute rate (% orders needing support intervention)

### Economics
- GMV/week
- Platform revenue/week
- CAC by channel (rough early, refine later)
- Support + ops cost per completed order

## 3) Phase plan + targets

### Phase 1 — April 2026 (Weeks 1–4): Pilot + proof
**Primary objective:** prove end-to-end reliability and marketplace liquidity within a small Lagos footprint (2–3 Local Councils).

**Targets**
- Supply: 40–60 onboarded handymen (across priority categories); 60% active
- Demand: 250–400 orders created; 150–250 completed
- Acceptance: 50% accepted within 2 hours; 80% accepted within 24 hours
- Completion rate: ≥60%
- Disputes: <5% of completed orders
- Repeat rate (30-day): 10–15% baseline

### Phase 2 — May 2026 (Weeks 5–8): Reliability + retention
**Primary objective:** increase repeat bookings and provider activation; tighten operational playbooks.

**Targets**
- Supply: 80–120 onboarded; ≥70% verified; ≥60% active
- Demand: 500–700 orders created; 300–450 completed
- Acceptance: 60% accepted within 2 hours
- Completion rate: 65–75%
- Repeat rate (30-day): 15–25%

### Phase 3 — June–July 2026 (Months 3–4): Scale Lagos + B2B expansion
**Primary objective:** expand to more Local Councils/LGAs and grow predictable B2B volume.

**Targets**
- 1,000+ completed orders/month
- 10–20 active B2B accounts
- Support cost per order trending down month-over-month

## 4) Phase 1 execution (week-by-week)

### Week 1 — Setup + supply cluster launch (2–3 Local Councils)
**Supply**
- Recruit and onboard 10–15 handymen/week (focus: plumbing, electrician, AC tech, cleaning first)
- Verification workflow operations (KYC-lite) with 24–48h SLA
- Handyman onboarding playbook and training session (accept → chat → confirm price → arrived → start/complete → photos)

**Demand**
- Local acquisition: estate groups, landlord/tenant associations, WhatsApp communities
- Early offer: wallet credit or platform fee discount for first completed job (pilot)

**B2B**
- Build pitch pack: “reliable handymen on-demand + escrow + reporting”
- Compile target list: property managers, estates, SMEs (salons, offices, restaurants)

### Week 2 — Activation + reduce friction
- Run weekly onboarding events (LGA-based)
- Publish price guidance ranges per category to reduce negotiation time
- Provider incentives for fast response (visibility/ranking)

**B2B**
- Close 2–3 pilot accounts with defined monthly job targets (10–30 jobs/month/account)

### Week 3 — Retention loops
- Customer referral credit (post-first-completion)
- Handyman referral incentive (verified-only)
- Quality enforcement: repeated no-shows/declines reduce visibility

### Week 4 — Review + expand coverage
- Expand to additional Local Councils with best demand signals
- Double down on top 3 categories by completion volume
- Publish pilot metrics (TTFA, completion, disputes, repeat baseline)

## 5) B2B track (starts Phase 1)
### Target B2B segments
- Estates / property managers (recurring maintenance)
- SMEs (salons, offices, restaurants) needing quick repairs and scheduled work

### B2B “product” (v1)
- Central account for the business (single payer)
- Monthly activity report: jobs, spend, ratings, completion
- Priority response rules (later)

### B2B funnel metrics
- Leads contacted/week
- Demos/week
- Pilot accounts/month
- Jobs per account/month
- Churn rate

## 6) Budget plan (₦800,000/month) — recommended allocation (Months 1–3)
- ₦250k: Handyman onboarding + incentives (field agents, verification support, transport)
- ₦250k: Marketing content + distribution (flyers, creatives, micro-influencers)
- ₦150k: Paid social tests (geo-targeted Meta; iterate weekly)
- ₦100k: B2B outreach (visits, materials, relationship-building)
- ₦50k: Contingency

## 7) Category rollout strategy
**Phase 1 (liquidity first):**
- Plumbing, electrician, AC tech, cleaning

**Phase 1–2 (retention + lifestyle):**
- Errands (fast “taskman” jobs)
- Hair stylist, nail tech

**Operational note:** focus on density in a few Local Councils before expanding category and geography.
